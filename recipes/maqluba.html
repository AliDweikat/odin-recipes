Maqluba

Home

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 lb chicken thighs (or lamb)
  • 1 large eggplant, sliced
  • 2 potatoes, sliced
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 cups chicken broth
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat vegetable oil over medium heat. Fry the eggplant and potatoes until golden brown. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add more oil if needed and sauté the onion and garlic until fragrant. Add the chicken (or lamb) and cook until browned on all sides.
  4. Add the chopped tomatoes, cumin, coriander, cinnamon, salt, and pepper. Cook for a few minutes until the tomatoes soften.
  5. Layer the fried potatoes and eggplant on top of the meat in the pot. Then add the soaked and drained rice on top of the vegetables.
  6. Pour the chicken broth over everything. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 30-40 minutes, or until the rice is cooked and the liquid is absorbed.
  7. Once cooked, let it sit covered for 10 minutes. Then carefully invert the pot onto a large serving platter to reveal the layered Maqluba.
  8. Serve hot with yogurt or a side salad if desired.
